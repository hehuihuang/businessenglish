<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>Small talk: Most interviews will start with a handshake and a little small talk. This may include questions about how you&apos;re doing, your travel to the interview, the weather, how your weekend went, and more. All you need to do is be polite and friendly. Keep your answers short, and feel free to turn the question back to the interviewer.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">寒暄：大多数面试都会从握手和简短的寒暄开始。话题可能包括你近况如何、来面试的路程、天气、周末过得怎样等。你只需要礼貌友好即可。回答尽量简短，也可以把问题反问回面试官。</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>For instance, if the interviewer asks how your weekend was, you might respond, &quot;It was great. We celebrated my mom&apos;s birthday. How was your weekend?&quot; Small talk is used to build a more comfortable environment before the interview begins. It may seem silly, but you can practice small talk on your own by asking yourself simple, non-personal questions. You could also practice with a friend.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">例如，如果面试官问你周末过得怎么样，你可以回答：“很好，我们庆祝了我妈妈的生日。你的周末怎么样？”寒暄的目的，是在正式面试开始前营造更轻松的氛围。它看起来可能有点“无意义”，但你可以通过自问一些简单、非隐私的问题来练习，也可以和朋友一起练习。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>Example: How are you doing? I&apos;m great, thank you. And you? I&apos;m doing really well. It&apos;s such a nice day out there. It is. The weather was perfect all weekend. Did you do anything interesting over the weekend? Yes, I went to the park with my family for a picnic. That sounds nice. It was. And you? I worked this weekend, but I got a lot done, so that was good. It does feel good to get work done!</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">示例：你最近怎么样？我很好，谢谢。你呢？我也挺好的。外面天气真不错。是啊，整个周末天气都特别好。你周末做了什么有意思的事吗？有啊，我和家人去公园野餐了。听起来不错。确实挺好。你呢？我这周末在工作，不过完成了很多事情，所以也挺好。把工作做完的感觉确实很爽。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>If you&apos;re preparing for an interview, practice talking about yourself and your work history. Record yourself if possible. Don&apos;t memorize a paragraph; practice speaking freely using a few key phrases. The more you practice before the interview, the more comfortable you&apos;ll feel answering questions during the interview.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">如果你正在准备面试，一定要练习用英文介绍自己和自己的工作经历。尽量录下自己的回答，控制在大约一分钟左右。回放时记下表达得很好的句子；如果有卡壳或遗漏，就写下更好的说法，第二天再练一次。不要背整段稿子，而是用一些关键短语去“自由表达”。面试前练得越多，面试中回答问题就会越从容。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>Since it&apos;s a simple question, think about your answer and practice it out loud before your interview. <div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">因为这是一个答案很明确的问题，所以你最好在面试前把回答想清楚，并大声练习。录音后回听：表达清不清楚？有没有发音特别难的词？把这些词单独拿出来慢速练习，注意口型和发音位置，再逐渐加快速度。把难词连续重复 10 次、20 次、30 次，建立“肌肉记忆”，面试时就更容易自然说出来。</div>

If you only practice in your head, it will feel much more stressful when you&apos;re in the room speaking for the first time. Record yourself and critique: what was unclear, and how can you say it more concisely?</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">可以用这句话开头：“五年后，我希望自己能成为/处于__。”一定要大声练习。如果你只在脑子里想，到了面试现场第一次开口会更紧张。把回答录下来并自我复盘：哪里不清晰？怎样能更简洁地表达？</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>RS: As I mentioned, I&apos;m interested in growth. In five years, I&apos;d like to be part of a company that is growing its business consistently. I&apos;d like to be in a Vice President role focused on development. And while I know that would require more public speaking, in five years I plan to be ready for it.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">RS：就像我刚才提到的，我很关注成长。五年后，我希望自己在一家业务持续稳定增长的公司里发挥作用。我希望能担任副总裁级别的职位，专注于业务发展。当然我也知道那会需要更多公开演讲，但我计划在五年后自己已经能够胜任。</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>TK: OK, I have everything I need. Do you have any questions for me about the company or the position? Common question: &quot;Do you have any questions for me?&quot; Up to now, the interview has been about whether you&apos;re a good fit for them. Now it&apos;s your turn to see whether they&apos;re a good fit for you. Your questions also show how well you understand the company and the role.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">TK：好的，我这边需要了解的差不多了。你有什么想问我的，关于公司或职位的吗？常见问题：“你有什么问题要问我吗？”到目前为止，面试主要在评估你是否适合他们；而从现在开始，轮到你确认他们是否适合你。你提出的问题也能体现你对公司和岗位理解得有多深入。</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>Before the interview, write down questions about the position, the company, or the work environment, and practice asking them out loud. You probably won&apos;t ask all of them; some may be answered earlier.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">面试前，把你关于岗位、公司、工作环境的疑问都写下来，并练习大声提问。你不一定会把所有问题都问完，因为很多内容在面试前半段就可能已经得到解答。</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>RS: Yes. Imagine you&apos;re looking back on this hiring decision in a year. The person you hired has exceeded your expectations. What did he or she do that impressed you most?</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">RS：有的。假设一年后你回顾这次招聘决定：你录用的人远远超出了你的预期。那么他/她做了什么，最让你印象深刻？</div>
---

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>TK: Great question. In one year, the person would have spent time learning from the team and long-time employees, then started making informed changes. I want the team to be a well-oiled machine: bouncing ideas off each other, coming up with new designs, and making headway into new markets. RS: That&apos;s helpful. It&apos;s good to know the expectations. Can you tell me a little more about the team I&apos;d be working with?</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">TK：好问题。我认为一年内，这个人会先花时间向团队和资深成员学习，然后基于充分理解再做出有依据的调整。我希望团队像一台运转良好的机器：彼此交流想法、产出新的设计，并在新市场上取得实质进展。RS：这很有帮助，知道你们的期待很重要。你能再介绍一下我将要共事的团队吗？</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>TK: Sure. They&apos;ve all been with the company for over five years and know the ropes. There&apos;s some frustration because of our lack of growth. This will be the third time we&apos;ve brought in a new team leader in four years. RS: Do you know what&apos;s causing that rapid turnover? T: Honestly, the last three team leaders were promoted from within. Sometimes it&apos;s hard to think outside the box when you&apos;ve been inside the box for a while.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">TK：当然。团队成员都在公司待了五年以上，很熟悉业务流程。因为公司增长不足，大家现在有一些挫败感。并且这会是我们四年内第三次引入新的团队负责人。RS：你知道为什么会有这么快的更替吗？T：说实话，前面三位负责人都是公司内部提拔的。有时候当你长期在“盒子”里，很难跳出固有思路去“盒子外”思考。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>We&apos;re hoping to bring in new ideas and energy and get our development team back on track. RS: That makes sense. I really appreciate that you&apos;re considering me for the position. I&apos;d love to be part of the company achieving its goals. TK: Very good. Thanks so much for coming in, Rachel. It&apos;s great to meet you. I&apos;ll give you a call in the next three days. RS: Sounds good. I look forward to it. Thanks again. TK: My pleasure. Notice that interviews often end with another handshake, and you may have a bit of small talk at the end as well.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">我们希望引入一些新的想法和能量，让研发团队重回正轨。RS：明白了。非常感谢你们考虑我这个候选人。我也很希望能参与到公司实现目标的过程中。TK：很好。Rachel，非常感谢你今天来面试，很高兴认识你。我会在接下来的三天内给你电话。RS：好的，我期待你的消息。再次感谢。TK：不客气。注意：面试结束时通常还会再握一次手，也可能会有一点简短的收尾寒暄。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>First, the handshake: today, men and women use the same handshake. Put your right hand forward and make it firm, but not too tight or aggressive. A firm handshake makes a good first impression.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">首先是握手：现在无论男女，握手方式基本一致。伸出右手，握得有力但不要过紧、不要显得咄咄逼人。一个坚定的握手会带来很好的第一印象。</div>

<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;"><strong>Posture: sit upright and look alert. Don&apos;t slump or slouch; that&apos;s too casual for a job interview. Don&apos;t sit stiffly either, but do sit upright with good energy. Facial expressions: keep your face engaged. Looking excited and happy sends the right message. In some cultures, being reserved may be more professional, but in the U.S., a friendly, engaged demeanor is important. Smiling and nodding show that you&apos;re engaged and listening.</strong></div>
<div style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7;">坐姿：要坐直、显得精神饱满。不要后仰瘫坐或含胸驼背，那会显得太随意；但也不要僵硬紧绷。保持端正，同时带一点积极的能量。表情：让面部保持“参与感”，看起来兴奋、愉快，会传递出良好的信号。在某些文化里，克制和严肃可能更专业；但在美国，友好、投入的气质很重要。微笑和点头能让对方感受到你在认真听、在积极互动。</div>
